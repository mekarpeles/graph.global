The World Wide Graph

The World Wide Graph (w2g) is a global database of unique entities (people, places, things) which spans and is accessible by the entire World Wide Web.

  • Any website in the world can connect to the Graph to tag and notify relevant people.
  • Get notified when you are tagged in any website accross the entire web.

Register your universal tag

Registration is secure and as simple as sending an email. We'll reply with a confirmation email to activate your tag.

Managing Your Tag

There are tons of advantages of having a tag in the Graph. Most of the advantages come from how you decide to configure it. You can customize when to be notified when you are mentioned, decide what info people see, and even endorse or dispute your tags. By default, your tags are displayed as "unverified" until you verify them.

Edits your account preferences is managed exclusively through email, so no password is required or will ever be leaked. Learn how it works.

Contributing to the Graph

W2g works similarly to Wikipedia. Anyone can suggest edits or register nodes, permitting they provide adequate proof. The w2g committee reviews each case for quality and accuracy. Want to be on the founding committee?

Privacy

W2g is a non-profit initiative and doesn't store any data private data you don't tell it to.

Using the Graph

Any website in the world can tap into the global graph. Doing so is as easy as importing a single javascript library, just like google analytics

Installation
<script type="text/javascript" src="/assets/graph.js"></script>
Referencing a tag
<cite id="?" class="w2g-tag">Michael Evan Karpeles (Mek)</cite>
